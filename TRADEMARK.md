# AssetVault trademark policy

The **AssetVault** name, logo, and related branding are trademarks of the AssetVault project maintainers. This policy applies to forks and downstream distributions of the open-source code.

## Permitted

- Refer to the software accurately (e.g. “based on AssetVault Pro Community Edition”).
- Use the name in factual statements, documentation, and academic work.
- Fork and modify the code under the [MIT License](LICENSE).

## Not permitted without written permission

- Publishing binaries, installers, or services named **AssetVault**, **AssetVault Pro**, or confusingly similar names in a way that implies official endorsement.
- Using official logos or icons as your product mark.
- Registering domain names or package names that suggest you are the official AssetVault project.

## Official distributions

Official builds, releases, and future commercial offerings may be distributed only by the project maintainers through channels they designate (e.g. official GitHub Releases or the project website).

## Fork naming

If you redistribute a modified version, choose a distinct product name and replace application icons in your own branding. Example: “MyStudio DAM (fork of AssetVault)”.

Questions: open a GitHub issue with the label `trademark` or contact the maintainers through the security contact in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).
